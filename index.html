Louis Chachere (1760 - 1827)

Charting an ancestral story from Revolutionary France to Southern Louisiana

By Victor Lowe

This is a story about the flight of a French immigrant to America in the late 18th century and how it can be used to understand the diversity of ancestral origin stories in America. While taking place over two-hundred years ago, the story of Louis Chachere (1760-1827) is at its core no different that people seeking a new life in the United States free from religious and political persecution.

His journey to Louisiana from France is just one ancestral case study out of millions throughout history that illustrates how any particular region in the United States has developed its diversity, history and culture.

Aristocratic Origins

The subject of our story, who’s journey will help guide us as an example of people of French origin laying down roots in Louisiana, was a man named Louis Chachere. Born around 1760 in France, not much is confirmed about Louis Chachere before he traveled to Louisiana, but much is speculated.

It is important to note that Louis Chachere’s story, or rather the story of how he became Louis Chachere, has largely been documented through oral history in addition to very few tangible records because he was fleeing Revolutionary France for safety and covering his tracks. Because of this, it is important to take everything that is known about him as well as everything that is known about the broader history of people leaving France for North America, and fill in the gaps to explain how people who undertook this journey helped to create the cultural makeup of Southern Louisiana.

Louis was speculated to be a member of the noble de Charette family, with notable cousins such as the famous counter-revolutionary general Francois de Charette, and it is even speculated that he was either a cousin, brother, or some sort of close relative to King Louis XVI.

Little is known about Louis’ life in France, but as a member of French nobility rumored to have familial relations to the upper echelons of French society and even royalty. Below are some notable suspected relations.

Louis was reported by various people to have been [A] a brother of Louis XVI and heir to the French throne [B] a Comte (a count) [C] a Baron. Whatever his origin, he certainly founded a dynasty of the new name that he adopted, for the Chacheres are now legion.

Excerpt from the Opelousas Daily World, “St. Landry History from the 1690’s”, July 29, 1982

François de Charette

1763-1796

Thought to be a ousin to Louis Chachere, Francois de Charette was a notable French General who served in the American Revolutionary War as well as was active as a counter revolutionary military leader. He assisted in defending Louis XVI during many attempts to kill or capture the royal family in 1792 and was later given charge of forces to overthrow the French Republic and reinstitute a monarchy, a feat which was unsuccessful and led to his capture and execution in 1796.

1845 William Greatbach illustration of de Charette courtesy of the National Galleries Scotland

Louis XVI

1754-1793

Louis XVI was the last King of France from 1774 until 1792, when the monarchy was abolished during the French Revolution, and executed a year later. Louis XVI is speculated to be a cousin or even half brother of Louis Chachere.

Illustration of Louis XVI courtesy of the National Galleries Scotland

de Charette Coat of Arms

The coat of arms of the de Charette family dates back to the 14th century in Britany and includes “A shield divided in 4 parts, gules with five golden bars, languin and crown of gules accompanied with three canners of sabl,” and “a silver ramping lion, accompanied by three birds with a long beak.”

Illustration by unknown artist, and description from Generation of Brittany (France): 1650-1912 Volume I, by Viscount Henry Frotier, 1912
